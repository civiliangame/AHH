# Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

**Criterion A:** Excessive anxiety and worry (apprehensive expectation), occurring more days than not for at least 6 months, about a number of events or activities (e.g., work or school performance).

**Criterion B:** The individual finds it difficult to control the worry.

**Criterion C:** The anxiety and worry are associated with ≥3 of the following 6 symptoms (with at least some symptoms present more days than not for the past 6 months). **Only 1 item is required in children:**
1. Restlessness or feeling keyed up or on edge.
2. Being easily fatigued.
3. Difficulty concentrating or mind going blank.
4. Irritability.
5. Muscle tension.
6. Sleep disturbance (difficulty falling/staying asleep, or restless, unsatisfying sleep).

**Criterion D:** The anxiety, worry, or physical symptoms cause clinically significant distress or impairment in social, occupational, or other important areas of functioning.

**Criterion E:** Not attributable to the physiological effects of a substance (drug of abuse, medication) or another medical condition (e.g., hyperthyroidism).

**Criterion F:** Not better explained by another mental disorder—e.g., worry about panic attacks (panic disorder), negative evaluation (social anxiety disorder), contamination/obsessions (OCD), separation from attachment figures (separation anxiety disorder), trauma reminders (PTSD), weight gain (anorexia nervosa), physical complaints (somatic symptom disorder), perceived appearance flaws (body dysmorphic disorder), having a serious illness (illness anxiety disorder), or delusional content (schizophrenia/delusional disorder).
